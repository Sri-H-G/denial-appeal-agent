MERIDIAN HEALTH PLAN
CLINICAL POLICY BULLETIN
Policy Number:      RX-114
Policy Title:       Biologic Disease-Modifying Antirheumatic Drugs
                    (bDMARDs) for Rheumatoid Arthritis
Original Effective: March 1, 2019
Last Revised:       January 1, 2026
Next Review:        January 1, 2027
Applicable Lines:   Commercial, Exchange
Review Type:        Prior Authorization Required
I.  SCOPE
    This policy addresses coverage criteria for tumor necrosis
    factor (TNF) inhibitors and other biologic DMARDs, including
    but not limited to adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab,
    golimumab, and certolizumab pegol, when prescribed for
    rheumatoid arthritis (RA).
II. COVERAGE CRITERIA
    Initial authorization may be approved for twelve (12) months
    when ALL of the criteria in RX-114.1 through RX-114.4 are met.
    RX-114.1  DIAGNOSIS
              Documented diagnosis of moderate to severe active
              rheumatoid arthritis, established or confirmed by a
              rheumatologist. Supporting serologic or imaging
              findings (e.g., rheumatoid factor, anti-CCP
              antibody, erosive changes) should be documented
              where available.
    RX-114.2  CONVENTIONAL DMARD TRIAL (STEP THERAPY)
              Documented trial of at least one (1) conventional
              synthetic DMARD - methotrexate, leflunomide,
              sulfasalazine, or hydroxychloroquine - for a minimum
              of three (3) consecutive months at a therapeutic
              dose, with inadequate clinical response.
              RX-114.2a  EXCEPTION - INTOLERANCE
                         Discontinuation of a conventional
                         synthetic DMARD prior to completion of
                         the three (3) month trial period
                         satisfies RX-114.2 when the medical
                         record documents a clinically
                         significant adverse effect necessitating
                         discontinuation. Documented adverse
                         effects include, but are not limited to,
                         hepatotoxicity, cytopenia, pulmonary
                         toxicity, or intolerable gastrointestinal
                         or mucosal effects.
              RX-114.2b  EXCEPTION - CONTRAINDICATION
                         A documented contraindication to all
                         conventional synthetic DMARDs satisfies
                         RX-114.2.
    RX-114.3  DISEASE ACTIVITY
              A validated composite disease activity measure
              documented within ninety (90) (90) days prior to the
              request indicating MODERATE or HIGH disease
              activity. Accepted instruments and thresholds:
                  DAS28-CRP    >  3.2
                  CDAI         > 10.0
                  SDAI         > 11.0
    RX-114.4  TUBERCULOSIS SCREENING
              Documented negative tuberculosis screening
              (interferon-gamma release assay or tuberculin skin
              test) obtained within twelve (12) months prior to
              initiation of therapy. Patients with positive
              screening require documentation of evaluation and
              treatment for latent tuberculosis infection prior
              to initiation.
III. CONTINUATION OF THERAPY
     Reauthorization may be approved for twelve (12) months when
     the medical record documents clinical improvement from
     baseline and continued treatment is recommended by the
     prescriber.
IV.  LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS
     Concurrent use of two or more biologic DMARDs is not covered.
V.   CODING
     J0135  Injection, adalimumab, 20 mg
VI.  REFERENCES
     American College of Rheumatology Guideline for the Treatment
     of Rheumatoid Arthritis. Arthritis Rheumatol. 2021.
